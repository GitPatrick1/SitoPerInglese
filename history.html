Introduction to AI History

The history of artificial intelligence (AI) dates back to the mid-20th century, when researchers first began to explore the possibility of creating machines capable of mimicking human thought processes. The term "artificial intelligence" was coined in 1956 during a workshop at Dartmouth College, where scientists like John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and Allen Newell laid the foundation for the field.

Early achievements focused on symbolic reasoning and problem-solving, with computers solving mathematical equations and playing games like chess. In the 1980s, the development of expert systems brought AI closer to practical applications, using knowledge bases to simulate human decision-making in fields like medicine and engineering.

However, progress slowed due to limitations in computational power and funding, leading to the so-called "AI winter." The resurgence of AI in the 21st century has been driven by advancements in machine learning, neural networks, and the exponential growth of data and computing power.

Today, AI plays a transformative role in industries ranging from healthcare and finance to autonomous vehicles and creative arts, making it a cornerstone of modern technological innovation.
